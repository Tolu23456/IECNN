IECNN

Iterative Emergent Convergent Neural Network

Core Concept

IECNN functions by generating many neural dots. Each dot independently produces a prediction. Through iterative convergence, stable and reliable outputs emerge from the collective agreement of many independent units.

Process

  1. Neural dots are generated in large numbers.
  2. Each dot makes its own independent prediction.
  3. The system evaluates all predictions.
  4. The most common or convergent results are identified.
  5. Convergent results are merged.
  6. Non-convergent predictions are discarded.
  7. The process repeats iteratively.
  8. Iteration continues until a stable pattern emerges.

Key Characteristics

Iterative Refinement Emergent Behavior Convergence-based Validation Inconsistency Pruning Stability as Stopping Condition

Data Capabilities

Designed to handle multiple data types:

Text Images Video Other Data Formats

Associated Mechanisms

AIM — Attention Inverse Mechanism

  • Dot predictions are transformed or inverted.
  • Attention is applied to focus on the most relevant parts of the prediction or context.
  • Generates novel, context-aware candidate solutions.

BaseMapping

  • A dynamic AI input representation system.
  • Converts text into maps of Bases, Modifiers, and Features.
  • Handles compression, phrase-level bases, and repeated words efficiently.
  • Outputs compact, structured matrices for IECNN processing.
  • Capable of discovering new bases after deployment.

Goal

To serve as a potential foundation for Artificial General Intelligence (AGI).
